## 7.10.3 Apparatus

The following equipment must be available for inspection: paint marker, pit depth gage, OD ring gage, drift, a light capable of illuminating the entire internal surfaces of the tool and its subcomponents, metal scale, tape measure, and a flat file or disk grinder. A calibrated internal micrometer is also required. See section 1.7 for calibration requirements for the micrometer. All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required.

## 7.10.4 Preparation

Record the tool serial number and tool description. Reject the tool if no serial number can be located, unless the customer waives this requirement.

## 7.10.5 Disassembly

When a tool is being qualified to maintenance classification A1, disassemble the tool and remove all internal components. All surfaces to be inspected shall be clean and all traces of thread dope and other foreign matter shall be completely removed from thread roots.

## 7.10.6 Fishing Tool Incidental Components

Visually inspect fishing tool incidental components such as springs, washers, nuts, bolts, pins, seals, and the like for wear and damage. If acceptance criteria for wear and damage are given in this procedure, use those given. If acceptance criteria are not given in this procedure but are given in the vendor's Inspection Procedure, use those criteria. If acceptance criteria are found in neither this procedure or the vendor's Inspection Procedure, the inspector shall reject any component which, in the inspector's opinion, may cause impaired tool function because of wear or damage.

If fishing tool incidental subcomponents are identified by part number or other descriptive marking, ensure that the part numbers or descriptive marks match the requirements outlined in the vendor's Inspection Procedure.

## 7.10.7 Common Inspection Methods Required

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for inspection of fishing tools must include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods:

- Visual Connection Inspection (7.14), except for tap wickers
- Dimensional 3 Inspection (7.16)
- Blacklight Connection Inspection (7.17) for all end and midbody connections
- MPI Body Inspection (7.19)

## 7.10.8 Visual Connection Inspection Exceptions and Additions

As mentioned in section 7.10.7, the fishing tool Inspection Program requires Visual Connection Inspection on all end and midbody connections, except end connections on tap wickers. Outlined below are some specific exceptions and additions to the requirements specified in Visual Connection Inspection.

### 7.10.8.1 Non-Sealing Shoulders on End Connections

A number of fishing tools, such as taper taps, box taps, rope spears, impression blocks, and magnets do not require a fluid seal at the shoulder. If it can be established with the customer that the fishing tool does not require a fluid seal, replace section 7.14.5.4 with the following: Non-sealing shoulder surfaces shall be free of raised metal or corrosion deposits detected visually or by rubbing a metal scale or fingernail across the surface. Filing to remove raised metal is permitted.

### 7.10.8.2 Tap Wickers

The wicker area of taps shall have no pulled or stripped threads within the catch area (as specified in the vendor's shop/assembly manual) and out to 2 inches on either side of the catch area. Wickers shall also be free of pits which appear to exceed 1/16 inch in depth or 1/8 inch in diameter, or which penetrate below the thread root, or which occupy more than 1-1/2 inches in length along any thread helix.

## 7.10.9 Dimensional 3 Connection Inspection Exceptions and Additions

As mentioned in section 7.10.7, the fishing tool Inspection Program requires dimensional inspection of the connections using Dimensional 3 Inspection method. Outlined below are some specific dimensional exceptions and additions to the requirements specified in Dimensional 3 Inspection.

a. Bevel Diameters. Fishing tools which will connect with tools having bit bevel diameters shall have bevel diameters within the ranges listed in Table 7.12.5d.

b. Stress Relief Features Required. End connections NC38 and larger on the tools so detailed in Table 7.3 shall be equipped with pin stress relief grooves and boreback boxes. Stress relief features are not required on end connections smaller than NC38.
