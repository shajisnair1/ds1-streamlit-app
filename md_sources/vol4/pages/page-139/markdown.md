# 7.32 Specific Requirements for Shop Maintenance of Rotary Table Master Bushings and Bowls

## 7.32.1 Scope

This section provides additional specific requirements for the shop inspection of rotary table master bushings and bowls.

## 7.32.2 Preparation

The following steps must be performed to prepare for the inspection of the bushings and bowls:

### 7.32.2.1 Verification of Customer Requirements

Determine from the customer the required master bushing sizes. If any of the equipment properties do not meet the customer requirements, do not proceed with the inspection; notify the customer.

### 7.32.2.2 Application of Alerts

The inspector shall review any product bulletins and safety alerts from the manufacturer for the tool inspected. All modifications required shall be completed prior to final inspection.

### 7.32.2.3 Preparation of Surfaces for Inspection

a. Critical area maps for each component shall be provided by the vendor to the inspector so that the proper inspection areas can be prepared for inspection. If critical area maps are not provided by the vendor, the entire tool shall be treated as critical and inspected on 100% of its area.

b. All foreign material such as paints, coatings, dirt, grease, oil, scale, etc shall be removed from the critical areas prior to inspection. A form of grit-blasting is the preferred method for preparing equipment surfaces for inspection. However, other methods capable of completely removing the foreign material from the critical areas can be used, such as wire brush wheels, soft buffing pads, steam-cleaning, and paint-stripping.

## 7.32.3 Inspection Requirements

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for the inspection of master bushings must include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods:

### 7.32.3.1 Apparatus

The following equipment must be available for inspection: paint marker, metal scale, tape measure, and spring caliper. All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required.

### 7.32.3.2 Traceability

Record the serial number and tool description. Reject the tool or assembly if no serial number can be located unless customer waives this requirement.

### 7.32.3.3 Common Inspection Methods Required

- MPI Body Inspection (7.19) with coverage as identified in 7.32.3.6.
- Liquid Penetrant Inspection (7.18) if necessary for proving up indications found using MPI Body Inspection.

### 7.32.3.4 Visual Inspection

Visually inspect the surfaces of the components to ensure no damages are present in manufacturer-defined critical areas of the master bushing and bowls, including the ID, taper, and pins. If damages are present, evaluate with the manufacturer's acceptance criteria. Unacceptable wear or damage (as defined by the manufacturer's acceptance criteria) in a critical area is cause for rejection.

### 7.32.3.5 Dimensional Inspection

In addition to the vendor's specified dimensional inspections, the following dimensional wear checks are also required for shop qualification of a master bushing. The dimensions listed below shall be measured, recorded, and compared to the manufacturer's allowable tolerances:

a. Master bushing ID at the top, 0.016 inch of wear is the maximum allowable unless otherwise specified by the manufacturer's dimensional requirements.

b. Master bushing ID at the throat, 0.063 inch of wear is the maximum allowable unless otherwise specified by the manufacturer's dimensional requirements.

c. Pin holes will be measured to verify that they meet the manufacturer's dimensional requirements.

d. When applicable, measure the diameter of the throat of bowls to verify it meets the manufacturer's dimensional requirements.
