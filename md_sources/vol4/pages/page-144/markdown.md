- Bolts: All bolts used on equipment that will be operated overhead shall have secondary retention installed and verified prior to function testing.
- Hinge and Latch Pins: Hinge and latch pins shall be verified to be the correct size for the application prior to assembly.
- Greasing Systems: Grease points and fittings shall be properly installed and the manufacturer-recommended lubrication applied.
- Signage: All warning, certification, and informational plates or signs shall be installed per the manufacturer's requirements for safe use.

## 7.34.5 Function and Fit Testing Requirements

The function and fit Testing Procedure developed by the vendor for tubular elevators must include the following items. Once the equipment has been fully assembled as per the vendor's procedures, a function test is required to ensure that the equipment is operating properly.

a. Tubular elevator assemblies capable of pneumatic or hydraulic operation shall be hooked up to a power unit capable of applying the same operating conditions that will be applied to the equipment while being operated on the rig. Mechanically-operated equipment shall be operated in the same manner that will be used on the rig.
b. A function test may consist of any number of cycles (as needed) of loads up to, but not exceeding, the rated load of the equipment under test.
c. The equipment shall be fit tested with the proper elevator inserts or bushings and a tubular mandrel that is the same size as the tubulars that will be handled. The OD of the test mandrel shall be measured with micrometers or calipers in at least 2 circumferential locations (90 degrees apart) at each end and in the middle and be within 3% of the nominal OD of the tubular size.

## 7.35 Specific Requirements for Shop Maintenance of Elevator Insert Bushings

### 7.35.1 Scope

This section will provide additional specific requirements for shop inspection of elevator insert bushings.

### 7.35.2 Preparation

The following steps must be performed to prepare for the inspection of the elevator insert bushings:

#### 7.35.2.1 Verification of Customer Requirements

Determine from the customer the required elevator insert bushing sizes and tapers. If any of the equipment properties do not meet the customer requirements, do not proceed with the inspection; notify the customer.

#### 7.35.2.2 Application of Alerts

The inspector shall review any product bulletins and safety alerts from the manufacturer for the tool inspected. All modifications required shall be completed prior to final inspection.

#### 7.35.2.3 Preparation of Surfaces for Inspection

a. Critical area maps for each tool's components shall be provided by the vendor to the inspector so that the proper inspection areas can be prepared for inspection. If critical area maps are not provided by the vendor, the entire tool shall be treated as critical and inspected on 100% of its area.
b. All foreign material such as paints, coatings, dirt, grease, oil, scale, etc shall be removed from the critical areas on the concerned parts prior to inspection. Any method capable of completely removing the foreign material from the critical areas can be used, such as grit blasting, wire brush wheels, soft buffing pads, steam-cleaning, and paint-stripping.

### 7.35.3 Inspection Requirements

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for inspection of elevator insert bushings must include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods:

#### 7.35.3.1 Apparatus

The following equipment must be available for inspection: paint marker, metal scale, tape measure, spring caliper, and elevator taper gauge (appropriate taper for bushings to be inspected). All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required.
