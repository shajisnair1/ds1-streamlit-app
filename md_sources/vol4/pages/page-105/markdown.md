## 7.8.2.6 Ring Gage Inspection

Check the cutter or roller diameter by sliding a ring gage over the length of the rollers and/or cutters. The gage thickness shall be 1/2 inch minimum and the gage width shall be 3/4 inch minimum. The gage inside diameter shall be the desired nominal diameter +0.005, -0 inch. The inside diameter of the ring gage shall be verified using internal micrometer specified in paragraph 7.8.2.1. The gage shall pass smoothly over the cutters or rollers. Gaps between the gage and the cutters or rollers surfaces shall not exceed 1/16 inch or the tool shall be rejected. For expandable tools, expand the cutters to their full diameter and repeat the test.

## 7.8.3 Assembly Requirements

The Assembly Procedure developed by the vendor for underreamers, roller reamers, and hole openers must include the following items:

### 7.8.3.1 Seals

Replace old O-rings and soft seals with new ones prior to reassembly.

### 7.8.3.2 Connection Makeup

Make up the midbody connections using the torque values required by the Assembly Procedure in accordance with section 7.30, Shop Makeup Procedure.

## 7.8.4 Function Testing Requirements

The Function Test Procedure developed by the vendor for underreamers, roller reamers, and hole openers must include the following items:

- Manually open and close moveable blades or arms at least three times.
- Rotate cutters and rollers.
- All moveable components should move freely without excessive play.

## 7.8.5 Post-Qualification Requirements

Clean and dry the connections and thread protectors. Apply thread compound and apply thread protectors. Place a 2-inch wide (±1/4 inch) white paint band around an acceptable component. The paint band should be 6 inches ±1 inch from the pin shoulder. The paint band should be 12 inches ±2 inches from the box shoulder for box × box components. Using a permanent paint marker on the outer surface of the tool, write or stencil the applicable DS-1 qualification class, the date, and the name of the company performing the inspection.
