## H

Heavy Weight Drill Pipe (HWDP). A group of pipes that are between normal drill pipe and drill collars in weight. They are characterized by the absence of an internal upset and the presence of an external upset about midway in the tube.

## I

Inspection. Nondestructive examination of a used component to confirm that it is ready to be reassembled into a tool or sub-tool.

Inspection Method. One of several possible inspection processes that may be applied to a specialty tool component. For example, "Blacklight Connection Inspection." In this method, a ferromagnetic connection is cleaned and magnetized, then covered with a liquid containing suspended fluorescent iron particles. The connection is examined under ultraviolet light to detect indications of fatigue cracks.

Inspection Procedure. A series of written steps that govern how the inspector is to perform an inspection method.

Inspection Program. A written outline that sets down the methods, procedures, and acceptance criteria needed to qualify all the used components in a specific specialty tool model.

Inspection Quality. The probability that an inspector, when he or she inspects a component, will correctly accept or reject it.

Inspection Report. A document that accompanies a component and shows the results of the inspection of that component. An important function of an inspection record is to demonstrate to an assembler that a component is fit for reuse.

Inspector. The person who performs an inspection.

Integral Tubing. Tubing with one integral pin connection and one integral box connection, with the joint typically externally upset near the box connection and possibly externally upset near the pin connection. An external upset creates larger ODs near the connections.

## J

Job order (or Work order). A document that specifies in detail the tool (or sub-tool) and final configuration that the Assembler is to produce.

## K

Key Variable. An aspect of an inspection procedure that is controlled by the inspection program designer to optimize inspection quality. Physical control of key variables will be in the inspector's hands at the time of inspection. However, the inspector may not have the information he or she needs to properly decide what the optimum values or settings should be. Therefore, the program designer, who will have such information, will guide the inspector by establishing values or ranges for key variables in the inspection procedure. For example, in Blacklight Connection Inspection, the optimum direction of the magnetic field is a key variable.

## L

Layout (verb). The act of setting out all required components, of verifying correct component part numbers against a Bill of Materials, and of verifying the presence of all required component records and other required documentation for the laid-out components, before assembling them into a specialty tool.

Limiting Component. The load-path component having the lowest calculated load capacity compared to all other load-path components in the tool.

Load-Bearing Component. A specialty tool component through which a load path passes.

Load Hold Period. The period in which proof loads are applied to a tool.

Load Path. The path through which loads are transmitted through a tool.

Load Path Component. A component through which loads are transmitted across or through a tool.

Load Rating (or Rated Load Capacity). The maximum load or combination of loads that a tool may be expected to safely carry in service.

Load Rating Methods. The procedures given in this standard for establishing load ratings on a tool.

## M

Maintenance. All those activities that are required to keep a specialty tool fit for continued service. Maintenance activities include disassembly, inspection of tool components, reassembly with new or qualified used components, and function testing.
