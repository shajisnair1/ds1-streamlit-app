# 7.9.5 Function Testing Requirements

The Function Test Procedure developed by the vendor for safety valves, kelly valves, and inside blowout preventers (IBOPs) must include a shell hydrotest and differential hydrotest per the requirements listed below.

## 7.9.5.1 Precaution, Preparation, and Equipment Requirements

Before applying hydrotest pressure, make sure that the part to be tested is isolated behind a barricade of sufficient size and strength to prevent injury in the event of a leak or shell rupture. Make sure all air is bled from the test system before applying pressure. Follow all other safety procedures established for the facility.

a. Block and bleed required. After applying pressure to the test piece in all of the tests below, the pressure source shall be isolated and the line pressure between the source and the test piece shall be bled to zero. Hydrotest time periods do not begin until these steps are accomplished.

b. Acceptance. The minimum pressure hold period for each test is 5 minutes. Any observable drop in pressure or water leakage through a valve body or around the valve operating stem, ball, or plug during the test period shall be cause for rejection of the valve.

c. Gage calibration. See section 1.7 for calibration requirements for pressure gages used for hydrotesting. Pressure measuring devices shall be calibrated to maintain $\pm 2\%$ accuracy of full-scale range. Pressure gage must be able to read at least $150\%$ of the test pressure but not more than $500\%$ of the test pressure.

## 7.9.5.2 Differential Test from Below

(Applicable to kelly valves, safety valves, and IBOPs)

a. Attach the lower (pin) end of the valve to the test fixture. The valve shall be in the closed position with no cap or plug on the upper (box) end.

b. Apply 200 psig from the lower (pin) end of the valve. Block and bleed the pressure source. Hold pressure for 5 minutes while examining the ball, operating stem, and midbody connection for leaks. Observe the pressure gage for pressure drop.

c. After the valve successfully passes the test at 200 psig, repeat the test as above at the full rated pressure of the valve.

d. Bleed pressure to zero.

## 7.9.5.3 Differential Test from Above

(Applicable to bidirectional kelly valves only)

a. Attach the upper (box) end of the valve to the test fixture. The valve shall be in the closed position with no cap or plug on the lower (pin) end.

b. Apply 200 psig from the upper (box) end of the valve. Block and bleed the pressure source. Hold pressure for 5 minutes while examining the ball, operating stem and midbody connection for leaks. Observe the pressure gage for pressure drop.

c. After the valve successfully passes the test at 200 psig, repeat the test as above at 5000 psig, or the full rated pressure of the valve (whichever is lower).

d. Bleed pressure to zero.

## 7.9.5.4 Shell Test

(Applicable to kelly valves, safety valves, and IBOPs)

a. Attach the lower (pin) end of the valve to the test fixture. The valve shall be in the open position with another valve, a cap or plug on the upper (box) end.

b. Apply the rated working pressure from the lower (pin) end of the valve. Block and bleed the pressure source. Hold pressure for 5 minutes, and examine the ball, operating stem, and midbody connection for leaks. Observe the pressure gage for pressure drop.

c. On kelly valves and safety valves, while maintaining test pressure, cycle the ball open and closed at least three times while observing the operating stem for leaks. No leaks are permitted. Leave valve in the open position. Bleed pressure to zero.

## 7.9.6 Post-Qualification Requirements

a. Clean and dry the threaded connections. Visually inspect thread and seal surfaces in accordance with paragraphs 7.14.5.2 and 7.14.5.4 to ensure damage did not occur from the test plugs during hydrostatic pressure test. Apply appropriate thread compound. Install thread protectors.

b. Attach a parts bag or similar to the valve. The parts bag shall contain at least one operating wrench (for safety valves and kelly valves) or opening tool (for inside BOPs). For tools so equipped, the parts bag shall also contain an allen wrench for removing the plug from the grease fitting hole and at least two grease fittings.
