## 7.32.3.7 Acceptance Criteria

Evaluate visual inspection, dimensional inspection, and NDT results against manufacturer's acceptance criteria. Indications identified by NDT may be relevant or non-relevant depending on their dimensions, shape, and location, as defined by the manufacturer.

- Relevant Surface Indications: Relevant surface indications are indications that result from discontinuities within the test part. Relevant indications are considered those with major dimensions greater than 1/16 inch (1.6 mm) and associated with a surface rupture.
- Non-Relevant Surface Indications: Non-relevant indications are indications not associated with a surface rupture that result from excessive magnetizing current, structural design, or permeability variances in the test parts. Any indication believed to be non-relevant shall be re-examined to determine whether an actual defect exists. If magnetic particle indications greater than 1/16 inch (1.6 mm) are believed to be non-relevant, they shall either be examined by the liquid penetrant method to confirm they are non-relevant or they shall be removed and re-inspected to confirm they are non-relevant.

## 7.32.4 Assembly Requirements

All warning, certification, and informational plates or signs shall be installed per the manufacturer's requirements for safe use.

## 7.32.5 Fit Testing Requirements

a. Master bushings shall be fit tested by completely assembling all components including installation of pins. Equipment may be disassembled for shipping purposes after a successful fit test has been performed.
b. Bowls shall be fit tested by installing them into an appropriate master bushing. It is the responsibility of the equipment provider to furnish an appropriate master bushing for testing.

## 7.33 Specific Requirements for Shop Maintenance of Rotary Slips

### 7.33.1 Scope

This section provides additional specific requirements for the shop inspection of rotary slips.

### 7.33.2 Preparation

The following steps must be performed to prepare for the inspection of the slips:

#### 7.33.2.1 Verification of Customer Requirements

Determine from the customer the required rotary slip sizes. If any of the equipment properties do not meet the customer requirements, do not proceed with the inspection; notify the customer.

#### 7.33.2.2 Application of Alerts

The inspector shall review any product bulletins and safety alerts from the manufacturer for the tool inspected. All modifications required shall be completed prior to final inspection.

#### 7.33.2.3 Disassembly

When performing maintenance on the tools to maintenance classification A1, the tool must be disassembled to the extent necessary that the manufacturer-defined critical areas of the load-bearing components can be thoroughly inspected.

#### 7.33.2.4 Preparation of Surfaces for Inspection

a. Critical area maps for each component shall be provided by the vendor to the inspector so that the proper inspection areas can be prepared for inspection. If critical area maps are not provided by the vendor, the entire tool shall be treated as critical and inspected on 100% of its area.
b. All foreign material such as paints, coatings, dirt, grease, oil, scale, etc shall be removed from the critical areas prior to inspection. A form of grit-blasting is the preferred method for preparing equipment surfaces for inspection. However, other methods capable of completely removing the foreign material from the critical areas can be used, such as wire brush wheels, soft buffing pads, steam-cleaning, and paint-stripping.
