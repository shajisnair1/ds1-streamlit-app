# 6. Transmittal

Transmittal involves sending both the tool and all the associated documentation to the field for operation. It is critical for the success of the tool that the customer has the right information about it.

# 6.1 Tool Transmittal Record

Every tool provided to a customer in accordance with this standard shall be accompanied by a Tool Transmittal Record. This may be a paper document or an electronic file; regardless, the information listed below must be accessible to the customer at the point of operation prior to using the tool.

## 6.1.1 Tool Identification

The Tool Transmittal Record shall give the serial number of the tool and describe how to verify the serial number in the field (e.g. provide a drawing showing the location of the tool's serial number).

## 6.1.2 Date and Shipping Information

The Tool Transmittal Record shall give the date and the location of the shop from which the tool was shipped to the field for use.

## 6.1.3 Maintenance Documentation

The Tool Transmittal Record shall provide a reference to the Inspection Records, Assembly Check Sheet(s), and Function Test Report(s) that have been generated for the tool since its last use. These references will, at minimum, include the date the work was performed, the location where the work performed, and the unique number (or other designation) used for each of these documents.

## 6.1.4 Waivers

Any approved deviations from the requirements of this standard or other customer requirements (as discussed in section 1.8) shall be communicated in the Tool Transmittal Record.

## 6.1.5 Assembly Configurations

The Tool Transmittal Record shall list the rig-end connections on the tool (if applicable) and any other assembly configurations.

## 6.1.6 Significant Dimensions and Weights

The Tool Transmittal Record shall provide to the customer's field crew a drawing or schematic of the tool showing all significant dimensions that are normally of interest to operations personnel. These will include at least the following if applicable:

- Measured outside diameter
- Nominal inside diameter
- Drift diameter
- Total weight
- Weight/foot
- Extended (and retracted) diameters
- Measured length
- Measured fishing neck diameter

## 6.1.7 Operating Instructions

The Tool Transmittal Record shall include operating instructions for running the tool.

## 6.1.8 Load Ratings

The Tool Transmittal Record shall provide the applicable load ratings for the tool, such as:

- Tension
- Torsion
- Compression
- Pressure (differential and/or hydrostatic)
- Temperature
- Combined (tables or curves)

## 6.1.9 Other Operating Constraints

Besides the load ratings, the Tool Transmittal Record shall also list any other operating constraints the vendor deems critical for ensuring trouble-free operation of the tool. These may include:

- Maximum hole curvature (rotating and/or sliding)
- Whether or not buckling is allowed (rotating and/or sliding)
- Maximum rotating hours
- Maximum rotating speed
