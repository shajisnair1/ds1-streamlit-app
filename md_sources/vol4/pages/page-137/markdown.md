## 7.31.3 Inspection Requirements

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for inspection of elevator links must include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods:

### 7.31.3.1 Apparatus

The following equipment must be available for inspection: paint marker, metal scale, tape measure, spring caliper. All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required.

### 7.31.3.2 Traceability

Record the elevator links pair serial number and tool description. Reject the components if no serial number can be located unless customer waives this requirement.

### 7.31.3.3 Common Inspection Methods Required

- MPI Body Inspection (7.19) with coverage as identified in 7.31.3.6.
- Liquid Penetrant Inspection (7.18) if necessary for proving up indications found using MPI Body Inspection.

### 7.31.3.4 Visual Inspection

Visually inspect the surfaces of the components to ensure no damages are present in the manufacturer-defined critical areas. If damages are present, evaluate according to the acceptance criteria provided by the manufacturer. Elevator links shall be visually inspected for excessive wear on the shank and eyes. Unacceptable wear (as defined by the manufacturer's acceptance criteria) on a critical area is cause for rejection.

### 7.31.3.5 Dimensional Inspection

In addition to the vendor's specified dimensional inspections, the following dimensional wear checks are required for shop qualification of elevator links. Dimensions listed below shall be measured, recorded, and compared to the manufacturer's allowable tolerances. If any dimension requires de-rating of the equipment, the tool shall be rejected unless waived by the customer.

a. Upper and lower eye thickness of each link.

b. Shank diameter (minimum diameter along the shank length) of each link.
c. Overall length from contact points of the eyes. Link sets up to and including 14 feet in length (as measured between the contact surfaces) shall match within 1/8 inch. Links over 14 feet in length shall match within 1/4 inch.

### 7.31.3.6 MPI Body Inspection Coverage

When performing the MPI Body Inspection (section 7.19) common inspection method, the inspection shall cover all of the critical areas for each load bearing component as defined by the manufacturer's critical area maps. If no critical area maps are available, the entire component shall be considered critical and inspected over its entire area. Recheck all critical areas with the magnetic field oriented perpendicular to the first field.

### 7.31.3.7 Acceptance Criteria

Indications identified by NDT may be relevant or non-relevant depending on their dimensions, shape and location, as defined by the manufacturer.

- Relevant Surface Indications: Relevant surface indications are indications that result from discontinuities within the component. Relevant indications are considered those with major dimensions greater than 1/16 inch (1.6 mm) and associated with a surface rupture.
- Non-Relevant Surface Indications: Non-relevant indications are inherent indications not associated with a surface rupture that result from excessive magnetizing current, structural design or permeability variances in the component. Any indication believed to be non-relevant shall be re-examined to determine whether an actual defect exists. If magnetic particle indications greater than 1/16 inch are believed to be non-relevant, they shall either be examined by the liquid penetrant method to confirm they are non-relevant or they shall be removed and re-inspected to confirm they are non-relevant.

### 7.31.4 Assembly Requirements

All warning, certification, and informational plates or signs shall be installed per the manufacturer's requirements for safe use.
