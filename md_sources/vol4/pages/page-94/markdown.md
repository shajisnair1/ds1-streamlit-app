## 7.7.3.5 Elastomer Compatibility Guidelines for Motors

Guidelines on compatibility of elastomer element with various types of drilling fluids, downhole temperatures, and pressures shall be provided in the Assembly Procedure. The vendor may use the proprietary name for standard or enhanced elastomers as long as this name is traceable to an elastomer type that can be proven compatible with the drilling fluid type and downhole conditions.

## 7.7.3.6 Stator and Rotor Fit Test Guidelines for Motors

A fit test for rotor and stator clearance or interference will be included in the Assembly Procedure. The Assembly Procedure shall specify the type of fit (clearance or interference), the location where the measurements shall be taken and a range of acceptable values for all expected drilling fluids and downhole temperature conditions. The dimensions obtained during this fit test will be provided to the customer (or the customer's representative) for documentation.

## 7.7.4 Post-Qualification Requirements

Clean and dry the connections and thread protectors. Apply thread compound and apply thread protectors. Place a 2-inch wide (±1/4 inch) white paint band around an acceptable component. The paint band should be 6 inches ±1 inch from the pin shoulder. The paint band should be 12 inches ±2 inches from the box shoulder for box × box components. Using a permanent paint marker on the outer surface of the tool, write or stencil the applicable DS-1 qualification class, the date, and the name of the company performing the inspection.

## 7.8 Specific Requirements for Shop Qualification of Underreamers, Hole Openers, and Roller Reamers

### 7.8.1 Scope

This section provides additional specific requirements for shop inspection, assembly, and function testing of underreamers, roller reamers, and hole openers.

Note: The inspection of the cutting structures of these tools is not covered in this volume. DS-1 Volume 5 (Bit Inspection Technical Standard) provides inspection procedures for reamer cutting structures. Customers wishing to standardize the inspection process for reamer cutting structures may reference Volume 5 as desired.

### 7.8.2 Inspection Requirements

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for inspection of underreamers, roller reamers, and hole openers must include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods.

#### 7.8.2.1 Apparatus

The following equipment must be available for inspection: paint marker, pit gage, a light capable of illuminating the entire internal surface, metal scale, tape measure, flat file or disk grinder, ring gauge. A calibrated internal micrometer is also required. See section 1.7 for calibration requirements for the micrometer. All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required.

#### 7.8.2.2 Preparation

Record the tool serial number and tool description. Reject the tool if no serial number can be located unless the customer waives this requirement.

#### 7.8.2.3 Common Inspection Methods Required

- Sub Inspection (7.12) if saver subs are present
- Visual Connection Inspection (7.14)
- Dimensional 3 Inspection (7.16)
- Blacklight Connection Inspection (7.17)
- MPI Body Inspection (7.19)

#### 7.8.2.4 MPI Body Inspection Coverage

When performing the MPI Body Inspection (section 7.19) common inspection method, the inspection shall cover the pins, arms, entire outside surface of the tool from shoulder to shoulder, and other load bearing members. Recheck all components except the outside surface of the tool with the magnetic field oriented perpendicular to the first (longitudinal) scan.
