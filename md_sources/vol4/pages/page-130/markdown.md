- 0.071 inch, +0.004 inch, -0.004 inch for 8-round threads on used connections; and
- 0.056 inch, +0.004 inch, -0.004 inch for 10 round threads on used connections.

e. Pitch Diameter and Ovality: For a connection compatible with tubing that has a nominal OD of at least 2-3/8 inches, an external thread pitch diameter gauge or an external thread taper gauge shall be used to measure the pitch diameter and ovality of a pin connection, while an internal thread pitch diameter gauge or an internal thread taper gauge shall be used with a box connection. A connection compatible with tubing that has a nominal OD of less than 2-3/8 inches shall not be measured for pitch diameter or ovality. If taper gauges are used, procedures (but not acceptance criteria) from API Technical Report 5TRSR22 shall be applied. If pitch diameter gauges are used, the external thread pitch diameter gauge shall be placed squarely against the nose of the pin connection with the pivot shoes on the crests of the threads, and the internal thread pitch diameter gauge shall be placed squarely against the face of the box connection. Using the lower shoe as a pivot, the pitch diameter gauge shall be oscillated through an arc to identify the largest and smallest readings. The pitch diameter gauge shall then be turned 90 degrees ±10 degrees, ensuring 360 degree coverage of the connection, to identify the largest and smallest readings. The average pitch diameter variation and ovality shall be calculated as follows and shall meet requirements in Table 7.53 regardless of which gauges are used to measure the dimensions:

- Average pitch diameter variation = (largest reading + smallest reading) / 2
- Ovality = largest reading – smallest reading

## 7.27 Drift Testing

### 7.27.1 Scope

This procedure covers the drifting of a component to detect any reduction in the ID.

### 7.27.2 Inspection Apparatus

a. A drift mandrel shall be used to perform drift testing.
b. See section 1.7 for calibration requirements for any electronic, dial, or Vernier device used to verify the OD of the drift mandrel. The measuring device shall also have flat contacts and be capable of measuring to a precision of 0.001 inch.

### 7.27.3 Preparation

a. The vendor of the component shall provide a drifting procedure that includes requirements for the OD of the drift mandrel, the length of the drift mandrel, the material that comprises the drift mandrel, and the depth to which the drift mandrel shall be inserted into the ID of the component. In addition, the following procedures are mandatory.
b. The drift mandrel shall be thoroughly clean and appropriate for use. The drift mandrel shall also be serialized with a unique identification number. It shall be visually inspected for any damages. The drift mandrel must have a constant OD and be continuous throughout its specified length. Disk and barbell-shaped mandrels shall not be used. The ends of the drift mandrel extending beyond the specified cylindrical portion shall be shaped to permit easy entry into the ID of the component.
c. When measuring dimensions of the drift mandrel, the mandrel and the measuring device used shall be within 10 degrees Fahrenheit of each other after being stabilized for at least 30 minutes to ensure accurate measurement.
d. The length of the cylindrical (measuring) portion of the drift mandrel shall be explicitly stated in the vendor's inspection procedures. The length of the drift cylinder shall be measured to the nearest 1/8 inch.
e. The OD of the drift mandrel shall be inspected at a minimum of four (4) locations: within 1 inch of both ends of the drift, within 2 inches of the middle of the drift and 4 inches from the end that is most likely to wear. At least two measurements at each location shall be obtained 90 degrees apart (for a minimum of 8 measurements) to a precision of 0.001 inch. The allowable minimum drift cylinder diameter shall be explicitly stated in the vendor's inspection procedures.
f. The drift mandrel OD shall be verified at minimum:

- At the start of each work order;
- After drifting 500 components;
- At the end of every shift;
- When requested by the customer or a designated representative;
