## 7.5.3.2 Connection Makeup

Make up the midbody connections using the torque values required by the Assembly Procedure in accordance with section 7.30, Shop Makeup Procedure.

## 7.5.4 Function Testing Requirements

The Function Test Procedure developed by the vendor for drilling jars must include the following items:

### 7.5.4.1 Trip Test

Cycle and trip the jars for upstroke and downstroke at least 3 times in each direction. Jars shall trip within the specified ranges given in the tool operating manual. Load testing equipment shall show evidence of calibration in accordance with section 1.7 at least annually.

### 7.5.4.2 Leaking

Hydraulic jars shall not leak oil during the test.

## 7.5.5 Post-Qualification Requirements

Clean and dry the connections and thread protectors. Apply thread compound and apply thread protectors. Place a 2-inch wide (±1/4 inch) white paint band around an acceptable component. The paint band should be 6 inches ±1 inch from the pin shoulder. The paint band should be 12 inches ±2 inches from the box shoulder for box × box components. Using a permanent paint marker on the outer surface of the tool, write or stencil the applicable DS-1 qualification class, the date, and the name of the company performing the inspection.

## 7.6 Specific Requirements for Shop Qualification of MWD/LWD and Rotary Steerable Tools

### 7.6.1 Scope

This section provides additional specific requirements for shop inspection and assembly of LWD, MWD, and rotary steerable equipment.

### 7.6.2 Inspection Requirements

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for inspection of LWD, MWD, and rotary steerable equipment must include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods:

#### 7.6.2.1 Apparatus

The following equipment must be available for inspection: paint marker, pit gage, a light capable of illuminating the entire internal surface, metal scale, tape measure, flat file or disk grinder. All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required.

#### 7.6.2.2 Preparation

Record the tool serial number and tool description. Reject the tool if no serial number can be located unless the customer waives this requirement.

#### 7.6.2.3 Common Inspection Methods Required

- Sub Inspection (7.12)
- Visual Connection Inspection (7.14)
- Dimensional 3 Inspection (7.16)
- Liquid Penetrant Inspection (7.18) for connections and tool components made from nonmagnetic material (Note: If the tool is constructed from ferromagnetic material, substitute Liquid Penetrant Inspection with Blacklight Connection Inspection, section 7.17, and MPI Body Inspection, section 7.19).

#### 7.6.2.4 Liquid Penetrant Inspection Coverage

When performing the Liquid Penetrant Inspection (section 7.18) common inspection method (or Blacklight Connection Inspection and MPI Body Inspection for ferromagnetic material), the inspection shall cover the end connections, the midbody connections, and the entire outside surface of the tool from shoulder to shoulder. Pay special attention to ports and machined areas. Any crack is cause for rejection.

#### 7.6.2.5 Visual Body Inspection

Visually examine the outside surface of the tool from shoulder to shoulder for mechanical damage. Any cut, gouge, or similar imperfection deeper than 10% of the adjacent wall shall be cause for rejection.

### 7.6.3 Assembly Requirements

The Assembly Procedure developed by the vendor for LWD, MWD, and rotary steerable equipment must include the following items:

#### 7.6.3.1 Seals

Replace old O-rings and soft seals with new ones prior to reassembly.

#### 7.6.3.2 Connection Makeup

Make up the midbody connections using the torque values required by the Assembly Procedure in accordance with section 7.30, Shop Makeup Procedure.
