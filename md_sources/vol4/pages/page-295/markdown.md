# Glossary

## Abuse
Operating a tool in a more aggressive wellbore environment than it was designed for, or that it might reasonably be expected to bear.

## Acceptance Criteria
The attributes of a used component that make it acceptable (or unacceptable) for reassembly into a specialty tool or sub-tool.

## Apparatus
The devices, equipment, and fixtures an assembler, inspector, or tester needs to conduct their work.

## Assembler
A person who assembles a tool or sub-tool.

## Assembly (noun)
A common oilfield expression to describe a series of tools or other drill string elements that are joined together. For example, “bottom hole assembly.” Except in Chapter 7, the word “assembly” is not used as a noun in this standard.

## Assembly (verb)
The act of joining together components to create a specialty tool or sub-tool. The word is used herein only in this context or as an adjective (as in “assembly area” below).

## Assembly Area
An area within the manufacturer’s facility that is set aside for layout and assembly of specialty tools.

## Assembly Check Sheet
A check list upon which the assembler records or checks off the tool assembly steps that are required in the tool assembly procedure.

## Assembly Drawing
A document that shows all the components of a specialty tool Bill of Materials in their final assembled state.

## Assembly Procedure
A written step-by-step guide an assembler follows for assembling a specialty tool or sub-tool.

## Authorized Representative
A person who is employed by or contracted by one party to represent the interests of another party during inspection, assembly, testing, or use of a specialty tool.

## Authorized Vendor’s Representative
A person who is employed by or contracted by the vendor and who operates or supervises the operation of a specialty tool.

## Auxiliary Component (or Auxiliary Sub-Tool)
A component or sub-tool that accompanies a tool to the rig but is not attached to the tool or run in the string with the tool. For example, a pump-down plug.

## B

### Bill of Materials (BOM)
A document that lists quantities, part numbers, and descriptions of all the components required to assemble a complete specialty tool or sub-tool.

### Bottom Hole Assembly (BHA)
An assembly of heavy drill stem components configured to accomplish certain tasks and placed at the bottom of the drill string. BHA components may concentrate weight on the bit, rotate the bit, measure drilling parameters and hole trajectory, steer the bit, or perform other functions.

### Buckling
The sudden axial collapse and lateral displacement of a drill string column that occurs when the forces that destabilize the column exceed the forces that stabilize it.

## C

### Component
A single piece that an assembler joins with others to form a specialty tool or sub-tool. A complex component such as an electronic circuit board may be fabricated from many parts, but it would normally be viewed as a single item by the assembler. A component would not be further reduced by a technician who was disassembling a tool, and doing so would result in its damage or destruction.

- Auxiliary Component. A component required for tool operation, but that is shipped and used separately from the tool. For example, a pump down plug.
- Expendable Component. A component that is always replaced with a new component upon disassembly and reassembly of a specialty tool.
- New Component. A component that has never been assembled into and used in a specialty tool.
- Nonconforming Component. A component that, during inspection, fails to meet the required acceptance criteria.
- Serialized Component. A component that has been assigned a unique serial number to differentiate it from other components of the same part number.
