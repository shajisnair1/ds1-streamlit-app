# 7.33.3 Inspection Requirements

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for inspection of rotary slips must include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods:

## 7.33.3.1 Apparatus

The following equipment must be available for inspection: paint marker, metal scale, tape measure, spring caliper, and a ruler. All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required.

## 7.33.3.2 Traceability

Record the serial number and tool description. Reject the tool if no serial number can be located unless customer waives this requirement.

## 7.33.3.3 Common Inspection Methods Required

- MPI Body Inspection (7.19) with coverage as identified in 7.33.3.6.
- Liquid Penetrant Inspection (7.18) if necessary for proving up indications found using MPI Body Inspection.

## 7.33.3.4 Visual Inspection

a. Slip Segment Bodies: Visually inspect the surfaces of the slip segment bodies to ensure no damages are present in the critical areas. Slip segments or carrier segments shall also be visually inspected for straightness by placing a straight-edge or scale on the tapered back of the segment, confirming that the straight-edge sits flush against the length of the taper and parallel to the axis of the segment. Any visible deformation shall be cause for rejection.
b. Slip Segment Hinges: Visually inspect the hinges and the hinge pins. If bending is present, evaluate according to the acceptance criteria provided by the manufacturer. Unacceptable damage (as defined by the manufacturer's acceptance criteria) on the critical area is a cause for rejection.
c. Slip Dies / Inserts: Visually inspect slip dies to ensure that all dies are the same type and size, and have the same orientation when installed in the tool. Dies with worn, chipped, mashed, or otherwise damaged teeth shall be replaced.

## 7.33.3.5 Dimensional Inspection

In addition to the vendor's specified dimensional inspections, the following dimensional wear checks are also required for shop maintenance of rotary slips. Dimensions listed below shall be measured, recorded, and compared to the manufacturer's allowable tolerances. Unacceptable wear (as defined by the manufacturer's acceptance criteria) on the critical area is a cause for rejection.

a. Slip contact length
b. Critical dimensions of body hinges and door (for powered slips), as defined by the manufacturer
c. Hinge pin diameters
d. Internal diameters of the guide plates

## 7.33.3.6 MPI Body Inspection Coverage

When performing the MPI Body Inspection (section 7.19) common inspection method, the inspection shall cover all of the critical areas as defined by the manufacturer's critical area maps. If no critical area maps are available, the entire component shall be considered critical and inspected over its entire area. The critical areas shall include all surfaces of any hinge pins, handles, and handle pins (excluding inserts, buttons, or cotter pins). Recheck all critical areas with the magnetic field oriented perpendicular to the first field.

## 7.33.3.7 Acceptance Criteria

Evaluate visual inspection, dimensional inspection, and NDT results against manufacturer's acceptance criteria. Indications identified by NDT may be relevant or non-relevant depending on their dimensions, shape, and location, as defined by the manufacturer.

- Relevant Surface Indications: Relevant surface indications are indications that result from discontinuities within the test part. Relevant indications are considered those with major dimensions greater than 1/16 inch (1.6 mm) and associated with a surface rupture.
