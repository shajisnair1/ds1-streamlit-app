## 7.29.3.5 MPI Body Inspection Coverage

When performing the MPI Body Inspection (section 7.19) the inspection shall cover the outside surface of all components including but not limited to the case from shoulder to shoulder, the piston, the chuck, and bit shank. Recheck all components except the tool structural case with the magnetic field oriented perpendicular to the first (longitudinal) scan.

## 7.29.3.6 Ring Gauge Inspection

Check the bit diameter by using "go" and "no-go" gauges. For accurate measurement, the temperature of the "go" and "no-go" gauges shall be within 20 degrees Fahrenheit of the bit shank after being stabilized for 30 minutes. Both the "go" and "no-go" gauges shall be fabricated from steel 1 inch in height with an OD equal to the nominal bit size plus 1-1/2 inch. The ID of the "go" gauge shall equal the nominal bit size plus 0.002 in clearance with a tolerance of +0.003 inch, 0 inch. The ID of the "no-go" gauge shall be equal to the minimum bit size (nominal size less maximum negative tolerance) minus 0.002 inch interference with a tolerance of 0 inch, -0.003 inch. The "go" gauge shall be carefully slid over the over the length of the bit with a force which does not exceed the weight of the ring gage. The inside diameter of the ring gage shall be verified using internal micrometer specified in paragraph 7.8.2.1. For acceptance, the "go" gage shall pass smoothly over the bit and inserts and bit and inserts shall not pass through "no-go" gauge.

## 7.29.4 Assembly Requirements

The Assembly Procedure developed by the vendor for air hammers must include the following items:

## 7.29.4.1 Seals

Replace old O-rings and soft seals with new ones prior to reassembly.

## 7.29.4.2 Incidental Components

Visually inspect all incidental components such as springs, washers, and the like for wear and damage. Any components with evidence of deformation, washing, or cracking shall be replaced.

## 7.29.4.3 Blow Tube

If a blow tube is present, it shall be pressed into place and not hammered. After seating it shall be inspected for damage. If the blow tube is found to be damaged it shall be replaced.

## 7.29.4.4 Oiling Requirements

All internal surfaces must be sufficiently oiled prior to assembly, with the same type of oil to be used on the rig.

## 7.29.4.5 Connection Make-Up

The Assembly Procedure shall specify the connection torques for midbody connections as well as placement locations for the tongs of the torqueing tool. As such, the torqueing tongs shall be placed at the location specified in the Assembly Procedure and made up to the torque numbers specified in the Assembly Procedure. See section 7.30 for required shop makeup procedure.

## 7.29.4.6 Air Flow Setup Guidelines

Guidelines for airflow setup including but not limited to check valve selection, adjustment, or shimming, choke sizing, and hydrocyclone configuration shall be provided in the Assembly Procedure. The airflow setup must be traceable to the drilling parameters associated with the expected application.
