b. All foreign material such as paints, coatings, dirt, grease, oil, scale, etc shall be removed from the critical areas on the concerned parts prior to inspection. Any method capable of completely removing the foreign material from the critical areas can be used, such as grit blasting, wire brush wheels, soft buffing pads, steam-cleaning, and paint-stripping.

## 7.34.3 Inspection Requirements

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for inspection of tubular elevators must include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods:

## 7.34.3.1 Apparatus

The following equipment must be available for inspection: paint marker, metal scale, tape measure, spring caliper, and a micrometer. All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required.

## 7.34.3.2 Traceability

Record the serial number and tool description. Reject the tool if no serial number can be located unless customer waives this requirement.

## 7.34.3.3 Common Inspection Methods Required

- MPI Body Inspection (7.19) with coverage as identified in 7.32.3.6.
- Liquid Penetrant Inspection (7.18) if necessary for proving up indications found using MPI Body Inspection.

## 7.34.3.4 Visual Inspection

Visually inspect the surfaces of the components to ensure no damages are present in the critical areas of the tubular elevator body, doors, latches, hinges, and the hinge pins. If damage or wear is present, evaluate according to the acceptance criteria provided by the manufacturer. Unacceptable damage or wear (as defined by the manufacturer's acceptance criteria) on the critical area is a cause for rejection.

## 7.34.3.5 Dimensional Inspection

In addition to the vendor's specified dimensional inspections, the following dimensional wear checks are also required for shop maintenance of elevator bodies. Dimensions listed below shall be measured, recorded, and compared to the manufacturer's allowable tolerances.

If any dimension requires de-rating of the equipment, the tool shall be rejected unless waived by the customer.

a. Hinge and latch pin diameter and the corresponding pin hole diameters.
b. Elevator link ear thickness of each ear

## 7.34.3.6 MPI Body Inspection Coverage

When performing the MPI Body Inspection (section 7.19) common inspection method, the inspection shall cover all of the critical areas as defined by the manufacturer's critical area maps. If no critical area maps are available, the entire component shall be considered critical and inspected over its entire area. The critical areas shall include all surfaces of any hinge pins, handles, and handle pins (excluding inserts, buttons, or cotter pins). Recheck all critical areas with the magnetic field oriented perpendicular to the first field.

## 7.34.3.7 Acceptance Criteria

Indications identified by NDT may be relevant or non-relevant depending on their dimensions, shape, and location, as defined by the manufacturer.

- Relevant Surface Indications: Relevant surface indications are indications with major dimensions greater than 1/16 inch (1.6 mm) and associated with a surface rupture.
- Non-Relevant Surface Indications: Non-relevant indications are indications not associated with a surface rupture that result from excessive magnetizing current, structural design, or permeability variances in the component. Any indication believed to be non-relevant shall be re-examined to determine whether an actual defect exists. If magnetic particle indications greater than 1/16 inch are believed to be non-relevant, they shall either be examined by the liquid penetrant method to confirm they are non-relevant or they shall be removed and re-inspected to confirm they are non-relevant.

## 7.34.4 Assembly Requirements

The Assembly Procedure developed by the vendor for tubular elevators must include the following items:

- Seals: Replace old O-rings and soft seals with new ones prior to reassembly.
