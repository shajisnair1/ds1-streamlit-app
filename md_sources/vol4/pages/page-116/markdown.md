## 7.9.2.2 Verification of Working Pressure/Trim

Record the tool serial number and tool description. If no serial number can be found, the tool shall be rejected unless waived by the customer. Verify the working pressure of the tool by one of the following means:

a. Permanent markings on the valve body.

b. Written records from the original equipment manufacturer traceable by unique serial number. (Information on valve body markings and on written records must agree or the valve shall be rejected.)

## 7.9.3 Inspection Requirements

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for inspection of safety valves, kelly valves, and inside blowout preventers (IBOPs) must include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods:

### 7.9.3.1 Apparatus

The following equipment must be available for inspection: paint marker, pit gage, a light capable of illuminating the entire internal surface, metal scale, tape measure, flat file or disk grinder. All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required.

### 7.9.3.2 Common Inspection Methods Required

- Visual Connection Inspection (7.14)
- Dimensional 2 Inspection (7.15)
- Blacklight Connection Inspection (7.17)

### 7.9.3.3 Blacklight Connection Inspection Coverage

Inspect end and midbody connections in accordance with procedure 7.17, Blacklight Connection Inspection. Extend the coverage of the blacklight inspection so that the entire accessible outside and inside surfaces of the valve are examined.

### 7.9.3.4 Visual/Dimensional Body and Internal Hardware Inspection

a. Visually examine the outside surface of the tool from shoulder to shoulder for mechanical damages. Any cut, gouge, or similar imperfection deeper than 10% of the adjacent wall shall be rejected.

b. Clean and examine the shell and internal components. Pitting, erosion, cuts, and gouges are not permitted on seal areas of the seats, balls, plugs, or operating stem. Pay particular attention to seal bores in the operating stem hole, and any area across which soft seals must move while the valve is operated under shell pressure. These areas must be bright metal with no evidence of pitting whatsoever.

c. Minimum tong space shall be 7 inches or the tool shall be rejected. Tong space may not contain any operating stem hole or midbody connection.

## 7.9.4 Assembly Requirements

The Assembly Procedure developed by the vendor for safety valves, kelly valves, and inside blowout preventers (IBOPs) must include the following items:

### 7.9.4.1 Seals

Replace old O-rings and soft seals with new ones prior to reassembly.

### 7.9.4.2 Connection Makeup

Make up the midbody connections using the torque values required by the Assembly Procedure in accordance with section 7.30, Shop Makeup Procedure.

### 7.9.4.3 Trim

For valves requiring H₂S trim, all components shall be verified as suitable for H₂S service by part number or serial number. Small parts, such as seals and springs, which do not carry identifying numbers shall be replaced with new parts which are known to be suitable for H₂S service.

### 7.9.4.4 Grease Injection

After assembling kelly and safety valves that have a threaded port for a grease fitting, temporarily install the grease fitting and grease the valve while operating the ball. Continue until grease is seen on the inside of the valve. Remove the grease fitting and install the appropriate plug in the threaded port. Operate the ball several times to confirm that it functions smoothly.

### 7.9.4.5 Position Indicator

Verify that the position indicator on the operating stem correctly reflects the open and closed positions of the valve.
