f. Other Threads: MUTs applied to connections not covered above (ACME threads, proprietary, etc) shall be obtained from the thread manufacturer.

g. MUT and Bevel Diameter: When possible, bevel diameters should be selected to optimize seal stresses (i.e. remain below a max of 100% of specified minimum yield strength, SMYS). If existing bevel diameters are not optimal, then fit-for-purpose MUTs should be used to ensure seal stresses do not exceed the SMYS of the connections while still providing the needed tensile and torsional capacity (which must be confirmed with the customer). These torque values and their justifications must be included in the Assembly Check Sheet.

## 7.30.3 Preparation

a. Calibration: See section 1.7 for calibration requirements for torque application equipment.

b. MUT Traceability: All MUT charts shall be labeled with the description and serial number of the made up components. Charts and graphs should be included in the Assembly Check Sheet.

c. Make and Break (M&amp;B): Breaking in newly re-cut connections using M&amp;B is recommended but not required. If a tool provider elects to perform M&amp;B, it should be performed at least 3 times at the recommended MUT. The connections should be made up slowly to avoid galling the threads. After the M&amp;B is completed (3 cycles), the connections shall be visually examined. M&amp;B at times may be cost prohibitive and involve extra handling risks. Consideration should be given to this before proceeding.

d. Cross Torqueing: Cross torqueing is not permitted. Cross torqueing is the action of putting components in a torque machine with multiple connections needing applied torque, and simultaneously transmitting torque across all connections. Cross torqueing is only permitted when vendor specifications require a cross torque.

## 7.30.4 Procedure

a. The MUT shall be verified prior to making up the connections as described in section 7.30.2. The basis for determining the MUT includes the connection type, box OD, pin ID, stress relief features, SMYS of material, and application. These connection properties as well as the MUT applied shall be recorded on the Assembly Check Sheet.

b. Thread compound shall be applied in a thin, even layer to all contacting surfaces (threads, shoulders, seals, etc) unless the manufacturer's recommendations state otherwise. Generally thread lock compounds should be applied to the thread surfaces but not seal surfaces; typical thread compound should be applied to the seal surfaces. In any case, the vendor shall follow the manufacturer's recommendations for coverage.

c. The friction factor of the thread compound used shall be recorded. Unless the vendor specification requires otherwise, the MUT applied shall be adjusted by the thread-compound friction factor (MUT used = Standard MUT × FF).

d. For any connections on which thread-locking compound is applied, the final torque values should be adjusted by multiplying by the corresponding friction factor. The friction factor and expiration date of the thread locking compound should be checked prior to application. This information is usually indicated in the packaging of the thread locking compound. If the information is unavailable, the thread locking compound should not be used except when the information is provided in the form of a Certificate of Conformance (COC) by the compound manufacturer.

e. Optional (may be required if specified by the customer): On shouldered connections that are made up hand tight prior to applying torque, an initial scribe line may be marked across the connection with a paint marker before applying the final MUT. After the MUT is applied, the scribe line should be offset. The MUT should be noted with a paint marker next to the offset scribe lines. Note: It is acceptable if these markings are later scraped off or covered during the final tool preparation.

f. On all connection types, once the final MUT has been applied and accepted per the applicable procedures, a torque stripe approximately 1 inch wide × 18 inches long shall be painted across the tight connection. The MUT shall be stenciled or marked near the painted torque stripe as follows: "Torqued to XXXX ft-lb." The torque stripe and MUT shall be clearly visible on the tool, after all preparations, so it can be verified on the rig.
