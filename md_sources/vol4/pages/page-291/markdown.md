# 8. Use

A specialty tool that is properly inspected, assembled, and tested according to this standard may still fail if used improperly. This chapter places requirements on both the vendor and the customer to help avoid these failures.

# 8.1 Vendor Responsibilities

The vendor is responsible for establishing and communicating the conditions and constraints under which a tool may be safely operated.

In many cases the tool vendor might not be the tool manufacturer. The load ratings, operating constraints, and operating instructions typically originate with the manufacturer of the tool. Regardless, the vendor is the party directly responsible to the customer for the performance of the tool, so this standard places the immediate responsibility for communicating load ratings, operating constraints, and operating instructions on the vendor. However, the vendor will often have to consult the manufacturer for advice on these matters.

## 8.1.1 Load Ratings

The vendor is responsible for establishing load ratings for the tool for every load type the tool is expected to encounter. These ratings may be determined using the methods given in Chapter 2 or by other suitable methods, as long as the vendor is confident that they are sufficient to avoid overload failures.

## 8.1.2 Other Constraints

The vendor is responsible for communicating any other constraints on the use of the tool that are important for reliable tool operation, as outlined in Chapter 6.

## 8.1.3 Operating Instructions

Unless the vendor provides an authorized representative to operator or supervise the operation of the tool, the vendor shall provide the customer with written operating instructions. These instructions shall be clear and unambiguous. They shall also be complete, in that they shall list every action the customer must take to ensure proper tool function. Likewise, the instructions shall list any action the customer must avoid to ensure proper tool function.

## 8.1.4 Communication

The vendor shall clearly communicate these ratings, constraints, and instructions to the customer prior to the customer's use of the tool. Listing them on or attaching them to the Tool Transmittal Record as required in Chapter 6 are the minimum actions required by the vendor to meet this obligation.

# 8.2 Customer Responsibilities

The customer is responsible for operating the tool in the manner prescribed and within the limits and constraints established and communicated by the vendor. This requirement is waived if an authorized vendor's representative supervises the tool's operation and the customer follows the representative's instructions and recommendations.

# 8.3 Continual Improvement

If a tool failure does occur during service, it behooves both the customer and the tool vendor to determine the cause of the failure and implement changes necessary to prevent reoccurrence. As such, customers, vendors, and manufacturers shall work together to expediently conduct failure analyses by sharing related information such as assembly reports, material test certificates, detailed well operation data, and providing access to the rig site during the investigation.
