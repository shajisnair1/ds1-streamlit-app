- At the end of each work order.
g. The drift mandrel and the component shall be within 10 degrees Fahrenheit of each other when the drift enters the ID of the component.

## 7.27.4 Procedure

Pass the drift mandrel through the entire length of each joint of tubing. The drift mandrel shall pass through the ID of the tubing freely using a reasonably-exerted force that does not exceed the weight of the drift mandrel. The drift should be inserted and removed carefully so that the threads are not damaged.

## 7.27.5 Acceptance Criteria

If the drift mandrel does not pass through the entire length of tubing, remove and clean the drift mandrel. Attempt the drift test again starting from the other end of the joint of tubing. If the drift mandrel does not pass through the entire length of the tubing on the second attempt, the component shall be rejected.

## 7.28 Ultrasonic Wall Thickness Inspection

### 7.28.1 Scope

This procedure covers the ultrasonic measurement of wall thickness using compression waves.

### 7.28.2 Inspection Apparatus and Calibration

a. The ultrasonic instrument shall be the pulse-echo type with a digital display.
b. The transducer shall have separate transmitting and receiving elements. Any transducer worn to a degree that light is visible under its face when placed on the reference standard without couplant shall be made flush or replaced.
c. Linearity Calibration: The instrument shall be calibrated for linearity over an interval of 0.100 inch to 2.000 inches after any repair of the instrument or at least every six months. See section 1.7 for calibration requirements.
d. The same type of couplant shall be used for both calibration and gauging.
e. The field reference standard shall be of the same acoustic properties as the component being inspected

and shall have at least two thicknesses that meet the following requirements:

- Thick Section = Nominal wall +0.100, -0.000 inch
- Thin Section = Nominal wall +0.000, -0.100 inch

f. The field reference standard shall be verified to be within ±0.002 inch of the stated thickness by a calibrated electronic, dial, or Vernier device. See section 1.7 for calibration requirements for this verifying device.
g. After field calibration adjustments, the gauge shall measure both thicknesses on the standard to an accuracy of ±0.001 inch.
h. Field calibration shall be verified at the following frequency:

- At the start of each inspection
- After each 50 inspected components
- When a measurement indicates a rejectable component
- Each time the unit is turned on
- When the gauge is suspected of having been damaged
- When probe, cable, operator, or tubing size or weight are changed
- Upon completion of each inspection

i. If the previous field calibration accuracy cannot be verified, all areas tested since the last valid calibration shall be re-gauged after correcting the calibration.

### 7.28.3 Preparation

a. All components shall be sequentially numbered.
b. The external surface where the readings are to be taken shall be cleaned to the bare surface.

### 7.28.4 Procedure

a. The parting line between the transmitting and receiving elements on the two-element transducer shall be held perpendicular to the longitudinal axis of the component.
b. The inspector shall scan the surface within a 1-inch radius of the lowest reading to confirm or modify that value. The lowest reading shall be recorded.
