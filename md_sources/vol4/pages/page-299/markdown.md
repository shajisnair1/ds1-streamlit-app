Structural Failure. A failure or malfunction of a specialty tool that involves a leak, the loss of any part of the tool or of the drill string below the tool, or results in sticking the drill string.

Sub-Tool. A device made up of two or more components that may be attached to other components or sub-tools to form a specialty tool. A sub-tool is not intended to be run without further assembly to other components or sub-tools to form a complete specialty tool.

**Temperature Rating.** The maximum temperature in which a tool may be expected to operate with no detrimental effects on its load rating, structure, function or ability to maintain a pressure seal.

**Tensile Load Path.** The path through which tensile load is transferred in a specialty tool.

**Tester.** The person who conducts a function test.

**Thick-Walled Drill Pipe (TWDP).** A class of drill pipe having thicker wall than normal weight drill pipe. Often used for heavy duty landing strings.

**Tool Family.** A group of specialty tools wherein the specialty tools have similar intended function and use.

**Tool Transmittal Record.** A document that accompanies a specialty tool to the rig, and includes the information required by rig personnel for proper running and operation of the tool.

**Torsional Load Path.** The path through which torsion load is transferred in a specialty tool.

**Traceable Component.** A serialized component for which documentation is available that links it to a specific heat number, heat treatment, and corresponding MTR.

**Transmittal.** The act of preparing a tool and tool records for delivery and delivering them to a rig.

**Trimming a Tool.** See “Dressing a Tool.”

**Type A Tool.** Type A tools and sub-tools are those intended to be used to perform some function, either downhole or on the surface, then to be retrieved, refurbished and used again. These are commonly referred to as “rental items.”

**Type B Tool.** Type B tools and sub-tools are those intended to be run once and remain permanently in operation. These are commonly called “sale items.”

**Used Component.** A component that was part of a specialty tool that saw field use. Upon return from the field and disassembly of the tool, a used component will often be reused for preparing the same or another tool for the field.

**Useful Load Capacity.** Rated load capacity divided by the drilling engineer’s design factor.

**Vendor.** The party that commercially rents, leases, or sells a specialty tool to a customer and that the customer will look to in the event of a failure. A customer may secure a specialty tool from a vendor singly, in combination with other tools and equipment, or packaged with some service. The vendor assumes the responsibility as a tool owner to incorporate any design changes or safety alerts communicated by the manufacturer.

**Work Order.** See “Job Order.”
