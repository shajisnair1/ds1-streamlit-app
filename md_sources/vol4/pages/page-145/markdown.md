## 7.35.3.2 Traceability

Record the serial number and tool description for the set of elevator inserts. Reject the tool if no serial number can be located unless customer waives this requirement.

## 7.35.3.3 Common Inspection Methods Required

- MPI Body Inspection (7.19) with coverage as identified in 7.35.3.6.
- Liquid Penetrant Inspection (7.18) if necessary for proving up indications found using MPI Body Inspection.

## 7.35.3.4 Visual Inspection

Visually inspect the surfaces of the components to ensure no damages or defects are present in the critical areas. If damages or defects are present, evaluate according to the acceptance criteria provided by the manufacturer. Unacceptable wear (as defined by the manufacturer's acceptance criteria) on the critical area is a cause for rejection.

## 7.35.3.5 Dimensional Inspection

In addition to the vendor's specified dimensional inspections, the following dimensional wear checks are also required for shop qualification of rotary slips. Dimensions listed below shall be measured, recorded, and compared to the manufacturer's allowable tolerances. If any dimension requires de-rating of the equipment, the tool shall be rejected unless waived by the customer.

a. Taper angle of the bushing's load shoulder. Measure and evaluate wear in accordance with manufacturer's acceptance criteria.

## 7.35.3.6 MPI Body Inspection Coverage

When performing the MPI Body Inspection (section 7.19) common inspection method, the inspection shall cover all of the critical areas as defined by the manufacturer's critical area maps. If no critical area maps are available, the entire component shall be considered critical and inspected over its entire area. Recheck all critical areas with the magnetic field oriented perpendicular to the first field.

## 7.35.3.7 Acceptance Criteria

Evaluate visual inspection, dimensional inspection, and NDT results against manufacturer's acceptance criteria. Indications identified by NDT may be relevant or non-relevant depending on their dimensions, shape, and location, as defined by the manufacturer.

- Relevant Surface Indications: Relevant surface indications are indications that result from discontinuities within the test part. Relevant indications are considered those with major dimensions greater than 1/16 inch (1.6 mm) and associated with a surface rupture.
- Non-Relevant Surface Indications: Non-relevant indications are indications not associated with a surface rupture that result from excessive magnetizing current, structural design, or permeability variances in the test parts. Any indication believed to be non-relevant shall be re-examined to determine whether an actual defect exists. If magnetic particle indications greater than 1/16 inch (1.6 mm) are believed to be non-relevant, they shall either be examined by the liquid penetrant method to confirm they are non-relevant or they shall be removed and re-inspected to confirm they are non-relevant.

## 7.35.4 Assembly Requirements

All warning, certification, and informational plates or signs shall be installed per the manufacturer's requirements for safe use.
