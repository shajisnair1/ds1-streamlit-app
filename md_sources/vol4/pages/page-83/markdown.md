# 7.6.4 Post-Qualification Requirements

Clean and dry the connections and thread protectors. Apply thread compound and apply thread protectors. Place a 2-inch wide (±1/4 inch) white paint band around an acceptable component. The paint band should be 6 inches ±1 inch from the pin shoulder. The paint band should be 12 inches ±2 inches from the box shoulder for box × box components. Using a permanent paint marker on the outer surface of the tool, write or stencil the applicable DS-1 qualification class, the date, and the name of the company performing the inspection.

# 7.7 Specific Requirements for Shop Qualification of Motors and Turbines

## 7.7.1 Scope

This section provides additional specific requirements for shop inspection and assembly of mud motors and turbines.

## 7.7.2 Inspection Requirements

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for inspection of mud motors and turbines must include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods:

### 7.7.2.1 Apparatus

The following equipment must be available for inspection: paint marker, pit gage, a light capable of illuminating the entire internal surface, metal scale, tape measure, flat file or disk grinder, and borescope. All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required.

### 7.7.2.2 Preparation

Record the tool serial number and tool description. Reject the tool if no serial number can be located unless the customer waives this requirement.

### 7.7.2.3 Common Inspection Methods Required

- Sub Inspection (7.12)
- Stabilizer Inspection (7.13), if the motor is equipped with a detachable stabilizer
- Visual Connection Inspection (7.14)
- Dimensional 3 Inspection (7.16)
- Blacklight Connection Inspection (7.17) for connections made from ferromagnetic material
- Liquid Penetrant Inspection (7.18) for tool components made from nonmagnetic material

- MPI Body Inspection (7.19) for tool components made from ferromagnetic material

### 7.7.2.4 Liquid Penetrant Inspection and MPI Body Inspection Coverage

When performing the Liquid Penetrant Inspection (section 7.18) or MPI Body Inspection (section 7.19) common inspection method, the inspection shall cover the entire outside surface of the tool from shoulder to shoulder. Additionally, welds and 24 inches (minimum) from both ends of the rotor shall be inspected. Any crack shall be rejected.

### 7.7.2.5 Visual Body Inspection

Examine the outside surface of the tool from shoulder to shoulder for mechanical damage. Any cut, gouge, or similar imperfection deeper than 10% of the adjacent wall shall be cause for rejection.

### 7.7.2.6 Borescope Inspection for Motors

Full length examination of the stator's elastomer element shall be performed using a borescope. The Inspection Program must include acceptance criteria for elastomer tears, chunking, and change in texture (discoloration). If the acceptance criteria are not specified, any tears, chunking, or change in texture (discoloration) of the elastomer element will be cause for rejection.

## 7.7.3 Assembly Requirements

The Assembly Procedure developed by the vendor for mud motors and turbines must include the following items:

### 7.7.3.1 Seals

Replace old O-rings and soft seals with new ones prior to reassembly.

### 7.7.3.2 Connection Makeup

Make up the midbody connections using the torque values required by the Assembly Procedure in accordance with section 7.30, Shop Makeup Procedure.

### 7.7.3.3 Ball Bearing Balls for Motors

Replace old balls from ball bearing races with new ones prior to reassembly or use new bearing assemblies.
