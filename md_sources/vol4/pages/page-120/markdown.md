- MPI Body Inspection (7.19) for tool components made from ferromagnetic material

## 7.23.3.4 Drift Testing for Surface Controlled Sub-Surface Safety Valves

As mentioned in section 7.23.3.3, the Inspection Program requires drift testing of the ID of the SSSV. In addition to the requirements included in Drift Testing (7.27), the minimum drift OD used to measure the ID of a surface controlled SSSV shall be no less than 0.030 inch smaller than the nominal ID of the surface controlled SSSV. For example, if the nominal ID of an SSSV is 3 inches, then the minimum acceptable OD of the drift mandrel shall be 2.970 inches and the maximum OD of the drift shall not exceed the nominal ID.

## 7.23.3.5 MPI Body Inspection and Liquid Penetrant Inspection Coverage

When performing the MPI Body Inspection (7.19) or the Liquid Penetrant Inspection (7.18), special attention shall be given to any sharp changes in geometry, such as shoulders, ledges, or portholes. If performing an MPI, recheck all components with the magnetic field oriented perpendicular to the first (longitudinal) scan in order to identify any longitudinal indications. If the transversely-oriented scan is performed with a Direct Current (DC) source and conductor, then the Residual Magnetic Particle Inspection Method (7.20) may be used. Any crack shall be cause for rejection.

## 7.23.3.6 Visual Body Inspection

Examine the outside surface of the tool body, between the end connections, for mechanical damage. Any cut, gouge, or similar imperfection shall be measured using the pit depth gauge. The adjacent wall thickness shall then be measured following requirements in Ultrasonic Wall Thickness Inspection (7.28). If the imperfection is deeper than 10% of the adjacent wall thickness, it shall be cause for rejection.

## 7.23.3.7 Borescope Inspection of the Hydraulic Piston Bore

A borescope shall be inserted in the piston bore(s) of the hydraulic system to visually inspect the bore for scarring, pitting, or any other imperfection that could affect the ability of the seal(s) in the bore to properly seal. If no specific acceptance criteria are included in the Inspection Program, then any scarring, pitting, or other damage shall be cause for rejection.

## 7.23.4 Assembly Requirements

The Assembly Procedure developed by the vendor for SSSVs shall include the following items:

### 7.23.4.1 Seals

Any sealing element, such as an O-ring or a soft seal, that is present in the SSSV shall be new during each assembly.

### 7.23.4.2 Connection Make-Up

The vendor's Assembly Procedure shall specify the correct method for the make-up of midbody connections, such as a torque value, make-up position, torque-turns, torque position, or torque shape to ensure proper make-up. See section 7.30 for the required shop makeup procedure.

## 7.23.5 Post-Qualification Requirements

Purge the inside of the valve with compressed air to remove the testing liquid. Clean and dry and entire valve, including the threaded end connections. Apply thread compound and thread protectors to the end connections. Place a 2 inch wide (±1/4 inch) white paint band around an accepted component. The paint band should be 6 inches ±1 inch from the pin shoulder. The paint band should be 12 inches ±2 inches from the box shoulder for box by box components. Using a permanent paint maker on the outer surface of the tool, write or stencil the applicable DS-1 qualification class, the date, and the name of the company that performed the inspection.

## 7.24 Specific Requirements for Shop Qualification of Frac Sleeves

### 7.24.1 Scope

This section provides additional specific requirements for shop inspection, assembly, and testing of frac sleeves.

### 7.24.2 Inspection Requirements

The Inspection Program developed by the vendor for the inspection of frac sleeves shall include the following procedural requirements and common inspection methods.

#### 7.24.2.1 Apparatus

A paint marker, a calibrated pit depth gauge, a metal ruler, a tape measure, a flat file or disk grinder, and a calibrated micrometer or caliper with precision to the thousandths are required for the inspection. All apparatus requirements specified in the applicable common inspection methods are also required. See section 1.7 for calibration requirements for the pit depth gauge and the micrometer or caliper.
