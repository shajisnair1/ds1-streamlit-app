## 7.33.4 Assembly Requirements

The Assembly Procedure developed by the vendor for rotary slips must include the following items when applicable:

- Seals: Replace old O-rings and soft seals with new ones prior to reassembly.
- Bolts: Measure all bolts used in slips and slip inserts that are load bearing with a bolt sizing tool to confirm that they match the length specified in the bill of materials.
- Slip Die Inserts: Ensure the die slot and load shoulders (if applicable) are clean before installing new slip die inserts.
- Hinge Pins: Ensure that hinge pins are properly installed with hinge pin retainers to prevent pins from inadvertently dislodging during use.
- Greasing Systems: Grease points and fittings shall be properly installed and the manufacturer-recommended lubrication applied.
- Signage: All warning, certification, and informational plates or signs shall be installed per the manufacturer's requirements for safe use.

## 7.33.5 Function and Fit Testing Requirements

The function and fit Testing Procedure developed by the vendor for rotary slips must include the following items. Once the equipment has been fully assembled as per the vendor's procedures, a function test is required to ensure that the equipment is operating properly.

a. Rotary slip assemblies capable of pneumatic or hydraulic operation shall be hooked up to a power unit capable of applying the same operating conditions that will be applied to the equipment while being operated on the rig. The power slips shall be function tested for a minimum of three (3) consecutive cycles. All components must function freely and without excessive play.

b. The equipment shall be fit tested with a test mandrel that is the same size as the drill pipe or tubulars that will be handled. The OD of the test mandrel shall be measured with micrometers or calipers in at least 2 circumferential locations (90 degrees apart) at each end and in the middle and be within 3% of the nominal OD of the pipe size.

c. When fit testing hand slips, the vendor shall provide the appropriate sized bowls for the slip type and tubular size that will be tested.

d. Fit testing shall ensure that the slips dies make proper contact with the test mandrel and the slip segments sit down completely in their bowl or housing.

## 7.34 Specific Requirements for Shop Maintenance of Tubular Elevator Bodies for Drill Pipe and Casing

### 7.34.1 Scope

This section will provide additional specific requirements for shop inspection of tubular elevator bodies. Elevator inserts and bushings are covered separately in 7.35.

### 7.34.2 Preparation

The following steps must be performed to prepare for the inspection of the tubular elevators:

#### 7.34.2.1 Verification of Customer Requirements

Determine from the customer the required elevator bore size and tensile rating. If any of the equipment properties do not meet the customer requirements, do not proceed with the inspection; notify the customer.

#### 7.34.2.2 Application of Alerts

The inspector shall review any product bulletins and safety alerts from the manufacturer for the tool inspected. All modifications required shall be completed prior to final inspection.

#### 7.34.2.3 Preparation of Surfaces for Inspection

a. Critical area maps for each tool's components shall be provided by the vendor to the inspector so that the proper inspection areas can be prepared for inspection. If critical area maps are not provided by the vendor, the entire tool shall be treated as critical and inspected on 100% of its area.
