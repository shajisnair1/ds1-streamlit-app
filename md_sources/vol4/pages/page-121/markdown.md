## 7.24.2.2 Preparation

Record the serial number and tool description. Reject the tool if no serial number can be located unless the customer waives this requirement.

## 7.24.2.3 Common inspection methods required

- Visual API Round Connection Inspection (7.25)
- Dimensional API Round Connection Inspection (7.26)
- Blacklight Connection Inspection (7.17) for connections made from ferromagnetic material
- Liquid Penetrant Inspection (7.18) for tool components made from nonmagnetic material
- MPI Body Inspection (7.19) for tool components made from ferromagnetic material

## 7.24.2.4 Visual Body Inspection

Examine the outside surface of the tool body, between the end connections for mechanical damage. Any cut, gouge, or similar imperfection shall be measured using the pit depth gauge. The adjacent wall thickness shall then be measured following requirements in Ultrasonic Wall Thickness Inspection (7.28). If the imperfection is deeper than 10% of the adjacent wall thickness, it shall be cause for rejection.

## 7.24.2.5 MPI Body Inspection and Liquid Penetrant Inspection Coverage

When performing the MPI Body Inspection (7.19) or the Liquid Penetrant Inspection (7.18), special attention shall be given to any sharp changes in geometry, such as shoulders, ledges, or portholes. If performing an MPI, recheck all components with the magnetic field oriented perpendicular to the first (longitudinal) scan in order to identify any longitudinal indications. If the transversely-oriented scan is performed with a Direct Current (DC) source and conductor, then the Residual Magnetic Particle Inspection Method (7.20) may be used. Any crack, regardless of orientation, shall be cause for rejection.

## 7.24.2.6 Activating Mechanism

If an activating mechanism, such as a dart or a ball, is used to activate the frac sleeve, the vendor's inspection procedures shall explicitly state the acceptable critical dimensions with tolerances of the activating mechanism, including but not limited to OD and length. In addition, any critical dimension of the seat(s) that affect(s) the ability of the activating mechanism(s) to function properly shall be measured. Each critical dimension shall be measured with the micrometers or calipers. If the OD of the activating mechanism is measured as a critical dimension, such as for a ball, it shall be measured in two locations 90 degrees ±10 degrees apart. If any activating mechanism has a critical dimension that does not meet requirements included in the vendor's inspection procedures, then the activating mechanism shall be rejected and replaced with another applicable mechanism, the critical dimensions of which shall also be measured prior to acceptance.

## 7.24.3 Assembly Requirements

The Assembly Procedure developed by the vendor for frac sleeves shall include the following items:

### 7.24.3.1 Seals

Any sealing element, such as an O-ring or a soft seal, that is present in the frac sleeve shall be new during each assembly.

### 7.24.3.2 Shear Screws and Set Screws

Verify that the type, head or body size, quantity, length, and material of any shear screws or set screws are included in the vendor's Assembly Check Sheet, required by Chapter 4. Ensure that the correct shear screws and/or set screws are installed based on the Assembly Check Sheet.

### 7.24.3.3 Connection Make-Up

The vendor's Assembly Procedure shall specify the correct method for the make-up of midbody connections, such as a torque value, make-up position, torque-turns, torque position, or torque shape to ensure proper make-up. See section 7.30 for the required shop makeup procedure.

## 7.24.4 Testing Requirements

The Testing Procedure developed by the vendor for frac sleeves shall include the following items:

### 7.24.4.1 Functional Testing

All necessary testing parameters, including the frequency of testing, shall be included in the vendor's Testing Procedure.

### 7.24.4.2 Testing Apparatus

The testing apparatus shall allow for pressure testing and access from above. The test fixture shall allow for pressure testing of the frac sleeve, access for the activating mechanism(s), and circulation throughout the test. In addition, since frac sleeves are often used in horizontal or nearly horizontal hole sections, the test fixture shall replicate actual field conditions as accurately as possible, including its orientation.
